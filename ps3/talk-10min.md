## The lens: deployed clinical AI

Three regions run different AI governing systems. We use deployed clinical AI as the test case: it has the richest peer-reviewed evidence base and is where the three systems diverge most sharply.

---

## What leaders predicted, and what's different now

Influential thinkers in China, the US, and Europe each forecast how the AI race would unfold. The 2025 open-weight surge confirmed some predictions and upended others.

---

# Policy

## Regulating clinical AI

FDA device-by-device, the EU's AI Act stacked on MDR/IVDR, and China's state-coordinated NMPA: three regulatory philosophies, read off the clinical-AI literature.

---

# Funding

## Capital versus reimbursement

The US dominates AI capital, but clinical AI is reimbursement-gated. The money that decides deployment is the payment pathway, not the venture round.

---

## How big is the US AI buildout?

A scale check: AI infrastructure spending as a share of US GDP, set against the country's great historical buildouts.

# Technical developments

## The chip war reshapes the race

Four years of US export controls, one abrupt reversal, and China's pivot to domestic silicon, the constraint that shapes everyone's technical strategy.

---

## Scale versus constraint: two paths to better models

The US answers the race with scale, more compute. China, capped on GPUs, is pushed toward method innovation.

---

## Open weights reach the bedside

The open-versus-closed split shows up fastest in medicine: China deployed an open reasoning model across hundreds of hospitals while US and EU LLM-clinical pathways sit in a regulatory void.

---

## The frontier standing: who actually leads

China stays remarkably close and wins on cost, but the absolute frontier is still American. Recognizing that matters.

---

# Landscape

## Who deploys clinical AI at scale

The US approves the most discrete tools, Europe regulates the hardest, and Asia (China-led) deploys at population scale.

---

## Real-world clinical AI, by region

The same split, in named systems actually in hospitals today: US commercial scale, Europe's autonomy milestone, China's population-scale screening.

---

## The ROI reality

Most enterprise AI pilots show no measured return, and clinical AI is mostly validated against expert labels, not patient outcomes.
